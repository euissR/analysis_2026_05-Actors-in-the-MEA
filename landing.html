Explore Middle East analyses:

Diplomatic outreach

How foreign powers are reshaping diplomatic ties across the region. From summit diplomacy and bilateral agreements to UN positioning and regional forum engagement, external actors are rewriting the rules of diplomatic engagement across the Middle East.

Soft power

Cultural influence, media, and education as tools of foreign engagement. State-backed broadcasters, cultural centres, and scholarship programmes are being deployed by competing powers to build long-term affinity and shape narratives across the region.

Economic power

Investment, trade, and infrastructure as levers of influence. Port concessions, energy contracts, and large-scale infrastructure projects are binding Middle Eastern states into webs of economic dependency that translate into lasting political leverage.

Security footprint

Military presence, arms transfers, and security partnerships. From permanent bases and naval deployments to arms deals and proxy force support, foreign powers are projecting hard power across the region with growing reach and ambition.

Explore Africa analyses:

Diplomatic outreach

How foreign powers are reshaping diplomatic ties across the continent. A new generation of embassies, summits, and bilateral frameworks is connecting African states to external patrons whose interests do not always align with those of the continent itself.

Soft power

Cultural influence, media, and education as tools of foreign engagement. From disinformation networks and state broadcasters to Confucius Institutes, aid organisations, and Gulf-funded religious institutions, soft-power competition is intensifying across Africa.

Economic power

Investment, trade, and infrastructure as levers of influence. Chinese infrastructure lending, Gulf sovereign wealth investment, and commodity deals from non-Western actors are reshaping African economies and creating new dependencies with geopolitical consequences.

Security footprint

Military presence, arms transfers, and security partnerships. Paramilitary deployments, French withdrawal, US base negotiations, and a surge in arms transfers from non-Western suppliers are redrawing the security map of the continent.
